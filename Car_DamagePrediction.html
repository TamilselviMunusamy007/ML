In the process of car manufacturing and car quality checks, damaged car parts like dents, cracks, and scratches may cause a significant risk to car safety and satisfaction. Car inspection, when done manually, requires a significant amount of time.

def extract_hog_features(image_path):
    pic = cv2.imread(image_path)
    pic = cv2.cvtColor(pic, cv2.COLOR_BGR2GRAY)
    pic = resize(pic, (128, 128))

    features = hog(
        pic,
        orientations=9,
        pixels_per_cell=(16, 16),
        cells_per_block=(2, 2),
        block_norm='L2-Hys'
    )
    return features

Feature Scaling

Technique Used: HOG (Histogram of Oriented Gradients)

HOG extracts structural features such as:

Edges

Shape distortions

Surface roughness

These are very critical indications of the presence of manufacturing defects such as cracks or dents.

Why not raw pixels?

Reduces the number of dimensions

Improves the performance of traditional ML models.

Prevents overfitting

def predict_damage(image_path, model):
    car_features = extract_hog_features(image_path)
    car_features = scaler.transform([car_features])
    prediction = model.predict(car_features)

    return "DAMAGED" if prediction[0] == 1 else "UNDAMAGED"

Benefits of Proposed Solution

The proposed method has

High Accuracy The SVM model built a model with a high prediction accuracy in determining damaged parts of a car.

Automation of Quality Inspection:

Removes manual checks for quality to reduce human errors.

Robustness to Variations:

Having the ability to deal with variations in images depicting different body parts of cars, including light variation, rotation, small noise, and so

Scalability:

The pipeline can be extended to handle bigger data sets and different types of car related tasks.

Data-Driven Insights:

Enables the evaluation of defect data to determine the points of focus for quality control.

Shortcomings

Limited Dataset: In the current dataset, only the examples of damaged images have been taken. This is the case since the undamaged examples had to be considered carefully.

Feature Dependence: The system relies on the HOG features. Even the slightest flaws might have the potential to be missed unless they are represented by the features.

Not Real Time Yet: The proposed solution is an offline solution, and additional work needs to be done for the solution to be fully integrated into the real-time production line.

Interpretability of the model: While SVM gives a good accuracy result, it is not an interpretable model compared to a Logistic Regression model. If any other method has to interpret the predictions given by SVM, then those methods need to work together.
